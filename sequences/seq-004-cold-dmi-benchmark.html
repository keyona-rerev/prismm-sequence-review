Part 4 — Succession Capture Deficit — Day 1
Single version
From Roland Mercer To {{first_name}} Subject The 15 percent of the score most banks are failing

{{first_name}},

We score a bank's exposure to inherited-deposit runoff across four components:

  • Actuarial exposure, 50 percent. Deposit-weighted depositor age.
  • Composition fragility, 20 percent. How concentrated deposits are in a narrow set of account types or households.
  • Market concentration, 15 percent. Reliance on a single branch footprint or region.
  • Succession capture deficit, 15 percent. Whether an institution has any operational capability to keep a relationship once the account holder dies.

That last one is the only component that isn't about depositor demographics. It's about process.

Private banking solved this decades ago with trust officers whose job is exactly that moment. Retail banking never built the equivalent, so on this component specifically, nearly every institution we've scored comes back weak, regardless of size or market. It's also the one part of the score fully within {{organization_name}}'s control to fix.

Send your FDIC certificate number and I'll have {{organization_name}}'s succession capture score back this week.

Roland

Why this version
Opens fresh rather than referencing a prior email directly, since this is now its own sequence launched weeks after seq-003 ended. Third metric anchor: succession capture deficit, all four components broken into a scannable list. CTA is a direct, concrete offer with a real timeframe.
