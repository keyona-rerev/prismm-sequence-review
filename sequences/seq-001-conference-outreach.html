Email 1 — First Follow-Up — Send within 48 hours
Version A — Direct & Clean
To {{first_name}} Subject From the {{event_name}}

{{first_name}},

We only had a few minutes {{event_day}} but I'm glad we crossed paths.

I'm building Prismm, a white-label digital vault that credit unions offer to members for estate and beneficiary planning, so when a member dies the institution is already embedded in what comes next instead of getting cut out of it. I'd love to hear how {{institution_name}} is thinking about that moment right now.

Open to a short call in the next few weeks?

Keyona

Why this version
Names Prismm and its value prop in one sentence. States intent clearly without being salesy. Best for operations, technology, and product-titled contacts.
Version B — Peer Energy
To {{first_name}} Subject Good to meet you at {{event_name}}

{{first_name}},

Wish we'd had more time to talk {{event_day}}, the cocktail hour moves fast.

I'm working on something called Prismm, a white-label platform that helps credit unions stay in the picture when a member dies and the family has to figure out what comes next. It sits at the intersection of estate planning and member retention, which {{role_relevance}} I suspect you've felt the gap firsthand.

I'd genuinely love to hear your perspective on how {{institution_name}} approaches it. No deck, just a conversation.

Keyona

Why this version
Positions as a peer rather than a founder in sales mode. Best for C-suite, VP-level, and relationship-oriented titles.
Use Version A for Operations, technology, and product-titled contacts
Use Version B for C-suite, VP-level, and relationship-oriented titles (Chief Experience Officer, VP Member Experience, CAO)
no response
Email 2 — Collaborative Invite — Send 10-14 days after Email 1
Version A — Warm Invite
To {{first_name}} Subject Quick question + something you might want to be part of

{{first_name}},

Following up on my note from a couple weeks ago, and also wanted to reach out about something separate.

We're putting together a short piece on how credit unions are navigating member retention across generations, specifically the moment a primary member dies and what happens to that relationship. We're pulling in perspectives from a handful of people in member experience roles and I'd love to include yours if you're open to it. One or two sentences on how {{institution_name}} thinks about that moment is all we'd need.

Happy to share the piece with you before it publishes.

Keyona

Why this version
"Something separate" resets the thread so she doesn't feel called out for not responding. Offering to share before publish is a genuine gesture of respect.
Version B — Specific Question
To {{first_name}} Subject For a piece we're publishing on generational member retention

{{first_name}},

Wanted to follow up on my note from a couple weeks ago, and also flag something that might be relevant to you.

We're publishing a piece on generational member retention and reaching out to a few VP-level voices in member experience for perspective. The core question is simple: what does your institution do, or wish it could do, at the moment a primary member dies and the family has to decide what comes next?

No more than a sentence or two from you. We'd send you the piece before it goes out.

Worth it?

Keyona

Why this version
The "wish it could do" framing gives them an opening even if the honest answer is "nothing." Works well for direct, data-oriented contacts.
Use Version A for Contacts who went cold quietly. Softer re-entry.
Use Version B for Direct or data-oriented communication styles.

Note: This email only works if the referenced piece is real or actively in progress. Do not send a collaborative invite for content that does not exist yet. Coordinate with content calendar before sending.

no response
Email 3 — Close the Loop — Send 14 days after Email 2
Version A — Light & Clean
To {{first_name}} Subject Closing the loop

{{first_name}},

I've sent a couple of notes and don't want to keep cluttering your inbox.

If the timing isn't right or it's just not relevant to where {{institution_name}} is right now, totally understand. If things shift down the road I'd love to reconnect.

Either way, glad we crossed paths at {{event_name}}.

Keyona

Why this version
Fully closes the sequence. Gracious and brief. The event callback is a quiet reminder this was not random cold outreach. Use as the default close.
Version B — One Last Offer
To {{first_name}} Subject Last note for now

{{first_name}},

Last note from me for a while, I promise.

If the piece on generational member retention is something you'd still want to weigh in on, the offer stands. And if a conversation about what we're building at Prismm ever makes sense, I'm easy to find.

Hope {{institution_name}} is having a good spring.

Keyona

Why this version
Keeps both doors open without pushing either. Use when the contact engaged at any point (opened, clicked, replied then went quiet).
Use Version A for Default close. No prior engagement signal.
Use Version B for Contact opened, clicked, or replied at any point then went quiet.

CRM Tags Required Before Starting Sequence
Tag Field Notes
{{first_name}} Contact first name Required. Used in every email.
{{event_name}} Full event name e.g. "the Filene dinner" or "FinovateFall"
{{event_day}} Day reference Game-time adjustment. "Tuesday" if same week. "Last week" or "a couple weeks ago" for Email 2.
{{institution_name}} Their organization e.g. "Lakeshore" not "Lakeshore Credit Union". Used in Emails 2 and 3.
{{role_relevance}} One-phrase role context e.g. "in a Member Experience role" or "given what you do at Lakeshore". Adjust per title.
After Email 3 — Ambient Only Until a Restart Trigger

Restart Triggers

  • They engage with Prismm content on LinkedIn
  • They change roles or institutions
  • Their institution announces a digital initiative or rebrand
  • They speak at or attend an event Prismm is also at
  • A mutual contact mentions them in a relevant context
  • 6+ months have passed and Prismm has a significant new development

When a restart trigger fires, treat the outreach as a warm re-introduction, not a continuation of the original sequence.
