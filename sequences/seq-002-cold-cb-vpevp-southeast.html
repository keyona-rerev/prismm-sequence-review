Numbers used in this sequence — sources

  • ~2.5 million Americans die each year (CDC, National Vital Statistics). Average household holds accounts at 2–3 financial institutions. The affected account volume is in the tens of millions annually — and largely untracked by the institutions holding them.
  • Attrition claim: We do not claim a specific attrition rate because no institution tracks it — that absence is the point. Do not fabricate a percentage.
  • Generational wealth transfer: $84 trillion expected to transfer between generations over the next 20 years (Cerulli Associates, 2023). Community banks capture a small fraction without intentional infrastructure.
Email 1 — The Hook — Day 1
Version A — Problem-first
From Roland Mercer To {{first_name}} Subject A category of attrition that doesn't show up in your dashboard

{{first_name}},

Community banks track attrition closely — rate sensitivity, digital competition, branch traffic. But there's one category almost no institution measures: what happens to a member relationship when the primary account holder dies.

The family doesn't know what accounts exist. Nobody at the institution reaches out. The relationship quietly dissolves while the family figures it out elsewhere. Roughly 2.5 million Americans die each year, each with accounts spread across multiple institutions. The attrition from that moment alone is significant — and almost entirely invisible.

Curious whether {{institution_name}} has a way of handling it.

Roland Mercer

Why this version
Names the problem without naming the product. Ends with a genuine question, not a pitch. The stat earns credibility without overclaiming. Best for ops and retail banking titles who live in attrition metrics.
Version B — Curiosity-first
From Roland Mercer To {{first_name}} Subject What {{institution_name}} probably isn't tracking

{{first_name}},

A question I've been putting to community banks your size: when a primary account holder dies, what happens to the relationship with the family?

Most can't answer it — not because they don't care, but because it's never been the kind of thing that gets measured. About 2.5 million Americans die each year, each leaving behind accounts at multiple institutions. For the family, whoever shows up first and makes it easy tends to win the relationship going forward.

Worth a brief conversation about how {{institution_name}} approaches it?

Roland Mercer

Why this version
Opens with a direct question that flatters their perspective. "Most can't answer it" is a subtle competitive pressure without being aggressive. Best for EVP and relationship-oriented titles who respond to peer framing.
Use Version A for VP Retail Banking, VP Operations, heads of member services — metric-oriented titles
Use Version B for EVP, Chief Retail Officer, Chief Experience Officer — relationship and strategy titles

CTA for Email 1: Soft — a question, not a meeting ask. The goal is a reply that opens the door for Email 2 to do harder work. Do not include a calendar link.

After Email 4 — Ambient Until a Restart Trigger

Restart Triggers

  • They engage with Prismm content on LinkedIn
  • They change roles or move to a different institution
  • Their institution announces a digital initiative, rebrand, or acquisition
  • They appear at an event Prismm is attending or sponsoring
  • A mutual contact flags them as newly relevant
  • 6+ months have passed and Prismm has a significant product update

When a restart trigger fires, treat it as a warm re-introduction. Do not resume the cold sequence — start fresh with a context-specific opener that references the trigger.
